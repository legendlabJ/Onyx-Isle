Experience

Eight recurring formats. An unlimited number of private commissions. A single set of standards.

The work spans international expeditions, executive sabbaticals, corporate and team retreats, university and student delegations, commissioned curricula, small invitation gatherings, an ongoing alumni circle, and one-of-one private engagements. Each format is built end to end. None of them is sold from a catalog.

The work

Curated expeditions

International travel programs for individuals and small groups across Africa, Asia, Latin America, the Caribbean, Europe, and the Pacific. The itinerary is designed to the traveler rather than to the catalog. Visas, vaccinations, ground transport, translation, accommodations, and the on-the-ground introductions are managed in full.

Executive sabbaticals

Structured time away for senior leaders whose strategic thinking has outgrown the environment in which it is being asked to occur. We design the destination, the rhythm of the days, the reading, the introductions, and the silence required to do the thinking the calendar does not permit.

Corporate & team retreats

Programs that deepen the cohesion of a team or rebuild trust within one. Facilitation, curriculum, and setting are designed so the operational shifts hold past the return flight and into the working quarter that follows.

University & student delegations

Cohort travel programs for undergraduate, graduate, and executive education programs whose curriculum needs the world to become larger than the classroom. Safety, intellectual rigor, and reciprocal cultural practice are non-negotiable.

Commissioned curricula

Trainings, workshops, and learning programs built to travel with a client into their own institution. Topics include cross-cultural practice, emerging technology, leadership, and field-specific curricula. Each engagement leaves behind materials the client can carry forward.

Convenings & salons

Small invitation gatherings designed for conversations that would not otherwise happen. Held at intentional intervals rather than on a recurring calendar. Frequently the quietest format we operate, frequently the most consequential.

The alumni circle

Ongoing membership for travelers and teams who have completed an Onyx Isle expedition. Periodic gatherings, curated reading, and the occasional return to a destination that has more to say.

Private engagements

Everything that does not fit the formats above. One-of-one commissions for institutions, families, and individuals whose requirements do not match a standard format and should not be asked to. By inquiry only.

Pricing is arranged on an engagement-by-engagement basis and is never the first conversation.

From the field

Three brief reports from recent expeditions. Details omitted where they would identify the people involved.

Dispatch No. IX
West Africa · Autumn

A team that arrived and a team that departed, not identical.

The brief was the usual one. A leadership cohort whose calendar had run slightly ahead of their relationships, in need of a week that would put the two back into phase. What we built around them had less to do with the itinerary we submitted and more to do with the hours we held open for nothing in particular. Evenings on a terrace. An afternoon walk with a person they had not expected to meet. A dinner whose menu was the least interesting thing about it. They returned with a decision that had been deferred for almost a year. I will not claim the trip produced the decision. I will claim that the trip is what permitted it.

— Principal, Onyx Isle
Dispatch No. VIII
Morocco · Winter

On the peculiar productivity of rest.

A sabbatical for a client who had arrived at the point where the question was no longer whether to take time away but whether time away could do what she needed it to do. We arranged the destination, the reading, the rhythm of her days, the hours she would spend with people, and the hours she would spend with herself. The trip was quiet by design. By the end of the second week she was answering the question that had sent her. By the end of the third, she was answering a better one. The letter that arrived a month after she returned is one of the things I keep.

— Principal, Onyx Isle
Dispatch No. VI
Senegal · Spring

The student delegation and the standing ovation we never saw coming.

Twenty-two undergraduates, two faculty, and a week on the ground that included lectures, workshops, meals with local scholars, and the kind of unstructured afternoons that earn their place on a university itinerary by producing what a classroom cannot. The moment that stayed with all of us was an unscheduled one, and I will not describe it here because the students deserve to be the first to tell that story. What I will say is that the ovation they received at the end of the week was not for the presentation they gave. It was for the way they had listened during the week that preceded it.

— Principal, Onyx Isle
